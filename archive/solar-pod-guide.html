Design Overview

The Solar Pod is a 29m¬≤ rectangular off-grid home with a sleeping loft and integrated 3.2kW solar array. It's the most technically complex design but offers complete energy independence.

Best For

Those wanting complete off-grid capability. Projects with good solar access (6+ hours direct sun). Households comfortable with energy management. Sites where rectangular footprint works better than circular.

Professional Installation Required

The solar/electrical components (panel interconnection, inverter, battery) require a certified electrician. Budget ‚Ç¨800-1,500 for professional installation of the electrical system.

Site Selection Requirements

Solar access is critical for this design. Site selection is more constrained than the other BioDome variants.

  • South-facing slope: Ideally 5-20¬∞ natural slope
  • Solar access: No shadows between 9am-3pm year-round (minimum 6 hours direct sun)
  • Water source: Spring, well, or rainwater within 100m
  • Level building platform: ~20m¬≤ relatively flat area
  • Upper terrace: ~10m¬≤ elevated area for sleeping loft (can be partially dug)
  • Drainage: Avoid low spots where water pools

Shadow Study

Before committing to the site, conduct a shadow study:

  1. Visit site on multiple days across seasons if possible
  2. Mark shadow positions at 9am, 12pm, and 3pm
  3. Check for trees, buildings, or terrain that cast shadows
  4. Remember: winter shadows are longer than summer shadows

Foundation & Wall Framework

Rectangular Gabion Foundation

  • Footprint: 5.0m √ó 4.1m rectangle
  • Height: 50cm above grade, 30cm below
  • Gabion mesh: Galvanized, 10√ó10cm openings, 3mm wire
  • Fill: Local granite, 10-25cm pieces (12-15m¬≥)

Wall Framework

  • Vertical posts: ~16 posts total (6 front, 5 each side)
  • Diameter: 15-20cm
  • Height: Front wall 3.0m, back wall 5.35m (creates roof slope)
  • Horizontal wattle: ~80 poles, 5-8cm diameter, 25-35cm spacing
  • Wall infill: Light straw-clay (front and sides), compressed straw bales (back/north wall for R-30 insulation)

Sleeping Loft (11m¬≤)

The loft adds 11m¬≤ of usable space without increasing the building footprint. It's designed for sleeping and storage, accessed by ladders.

Loft Specifications

  • Floor area: 2 compartments √ó ~5.5m¬≤ each = ~11m¬≤ total
  • Floor height: 3.0m above main floor
  • Ceiling height: 2.0m (comfortable sitting/dressing)
  • Depth: 2.5m (extends into upper terrace)
  • Width per compartment: ~2.1m

Loft Structure

  • Floor joists: 6 poles, 12-15cm diameter, spanning 4.1m front-to-back
  • Decking: Rough planks or split poles
  • Divider wall: Partial height (1.8m) between compartments for light sharing
  • Ladder access: One per compartment, ~60¬∞ angle, 2.5m climb

Back Wall Insulation (R-30)

The north-facing back wall uses compressed straw bales (450mm thick):

  • R-value: R-30 (excellent thermal performance)
  • Fire resistance: Dense straw won't burn; clay plaster coating prevents flame spread
  • Assembly: 1 bale high + 50mm lime plaster on both sides

Roof Structure (Solar-Optimized)

  • Pitch: 30¬∞ (optimal for solar in Portugal)

Purlins (Solar Mount Support)

  • Diameter: 10-12cm eucalyptus

Test Before Solar Installation

Do NOT install solar panels on a roof that hasn't been water-tested. A single leak will damage electrical components. Use a hose to simulate rain for 48 hours, inspect from inside for drips.

The Solar System

Complete guide to off-grid power: panels, inverter, battery, wiring

System Architecture (Sun to Light Switch)

  1. Sun hits 8 √ó 400W panels (3.2kW array) ‚Üí DC electricity
  2. DC wiring (UV-resistant conduit) ‚Üí routes to inverter
  3. Hybrid inverter converts DC to AC (230V household voltage)
  4. AC wiring ‚Üí powers lights, refrigerator, etc. OR charges battery
  5. Battery stores excess energy (5-10kWh LiFePO4)
  6. Backup generator kicks in if battery depletes

8 Solar Panels (400W Each)

  • Dimensions: 2382mm √ó 1134mm √ó 30mm (door-sized)
  • Weight: ~22kg each (manageable by 2 people)
  • Type: Monocrystalline (20-22% efficiency)
  • Configuration: 2 strings of 4 panels in series
  • Result: ~80V DC, ~25 amps

DC Wiring and Conduit Routing

  • Panels on roof ‚Üí junction boxes ‚Üí UV-resistant conduit down slope
  • Down north wall ‚Üí into building to inverter location
  • Wire sizing: 4mm¬≤ solar-rated cable (for 25-amp system)
  • Grounding: Green/bare copper runs parallel
  • Conduit clips: Every 2-3 feet securing to roof structure

Battery Storage (5-10kWh LiFePO4)

How much battery? 2-3 days autonomy = 6kWh daily load √ó 2 days √∑ 0.8 safe discharge = 15kWh needed. Budget for 5-10kWh initially, expandable.

  • Chemistry: Lithium Iron Phosphate (10,000+ cycles, ~10-15 years)
  • Efficiency: 96-98% round-trip
  • Safety: More stable than other lithium chemistries
  • Temperature: Works -10 to +55¬∞C

Grounding (Earth Safety)

  • Copper ground rod: 3m long, driven into moist earth
  • Grounding conductor: 4mm¬≤ copper from rod to main panel and inverter
  • Testing: Measure resistance (should be under 25 ohms)

Expected Power Generation

  • Sunny day: 12-15kWh generation, 6kWh use, 6-9kWh stored in battery
  • Cloudy day: 3-5kWh generation, battery covers deficit
  • Multi-day rain: Battery depletes; generator kicks in
  • Winter months: Shorter days, lower sun angle; battery stores less

Realistic Daily Load (6kWh)

  • Refrigerator: ~2.5kWh/day (non-negotiable)
  • Lighting (LED): ~0.5kWh/day
  • Water heating (solar thermal + backup): ~2kWh/day
  • Internet/devices: ~0.3kWh/day
  • Cooking (induction, sunny hours): ~1kWh/day

Construction Phases

22-week timeline with integrated solar installation

0

Site Selection & Layout (Week 1)

  • Confirm south-facing slope with compass
  • Shadow study: Mark shadow positions at 9am, 12pm, 3pm
  • Stake building corners (5.0m √ó 4.1m rectangle)
  • Stake upper terrace area for loft
1

Pole Harvest & Treatment (Weeks 1-5)

  • Fell ~130 eucalyptus trees
  • Debark within 48 hours
  • Prioritize posts (16 pcs) and rafters (5 pcs)
  • Set up borax treatment tank, dry 2-3 weeks
2

Earthworks & Foundation (Weeks 3-6)

  • Cut upper terrace platform for loft
  • Excavate rectangular footprint (5.0m √ó 4.1m, 30cm deep)
  • Assemble gabion mesh cages
  • Fill with granite (12-15m¬≥), level top course
3

Wall Framework (Weeks 6-8)

  • Char and oil 16 perimeter posts
  • Set posts (back posts are taller: 5.35m vs 3.0m front)
  • Weave horizontal wattle (~80 poles)
4

Loft Framing (Weeks 8-9)

  • Install loft floor joists (6 pcs, spanning 4.1m)
  • Deck loft with rough planks
  • Frame loft compartment divider
  • Install ladder access points
  • Test load capacity before proceeding
5

Roof Structure (Weeks 9-10)

  • Install 5 parallel rafters (30¬∞ pitch)
  • Install 3 purlins for solar mount support
  • Pre-plan solar panel mounting points
6

Roof Weatherproofing (Weeks 10-11)

  • Install cork board sheathing
  • Lay EPDM membrane (1mm), seal all seams
  • Install drainage mat
  • Water test with hose for 48 hours
7

Solar Installation (Weeks 11-12) ‚Äî Professional

  • Mount aluminum rails to purlins
  • Install 8 √ó 400W panels in 2 strings
  • Run DC conduit from panels to interior
  • Install disconnects and junction boxes
  • Ground system with 3m copper rod
  • Must hire: Certified solar electrician
8

Electrical Completion (Weeks 12-13) ‚Äî Professional

  • Mount inverter in interior closet (north wall)
  • Install battery (5-10kWh LiFePO4)
  • Run AC distribution panel with breakers
  • Test full system under load
  • Must hire: Licensed electrician
9

Wall Infill (Weeks 13-17)

  • Light straw-clay infill on front and side walls
  • Compressed straw bales on north back wall (R-30)
  • Allow 4-6 weeks drying
10

Finishing (Weeks 17-22)

  • Lime render (base coat, cure 2 weeks, finish coat)
  • Install clerestory window (5.0m √ó 2.3m)
  • Install side windows and entry door
  • Floor construction
  • Final electrical testing

Floor Plan & Spatial Organization

Main Floor (18m¬≤)

  • Entry/mud room (2m¬≤): East side, door location, coat hooks, shoe storage
  • Kitchen (5m¬≤): Along east wall adjacent to entry, counter runs north-south
  • Living area (8m¬≤): Center and west side, maximum window exposure
  • Bathroom (3m¬≤): Northeast corner under loft overhang

Loft Level (11m¬≤)

  • Sleeping compartment A (5.5m¬≤): West side, fits 2 single or 1 double bed
  • Sleeping compartment B (5.5m¬≤): East side, mirror of Compartment A
  • Divider wall: Partial height (1.8m) allows light sharing

Systems Integration

  • Inverter & Battery: Interior closet on north wall (~1.2m √ó 0.8m)
  • DC conduit: Enters through north wall at ridge level
  • Air circulation: Clerestory provides stack ventilation

Clerestory Window (5.0m √ó 2.3m)

  • Location: Full-width north wall, where roof meets loft ceiling
  • Purpose: Diffuse north light (no glare), stack-effect ventilation
  • Design: Fixed panes with operable transoms
Summer Ventilation Critical

Central Portugal routinely exceeds 40¬∞C in summer. Required ventilation features:

  • Cross-ventilation: Operable windows on both east and west walls
  • Clerestory operation: Must be operable, not fixed glazing
  • Loft vents: Add ventilated gable ends or soffit vents
  • Thermal mass strategy: Open windows overnight, close by 10am

Maintenance Schedule

Solar System

  • Monthly: Check inverter display for error codes
  • Quarterly: Clean panels if dusty (soft brush, water)
  • Annually: Inspect all wiring connections, check battery health
  • Every 5 years: Professional electrical inspection
  • At 10-15 years: Battery replacement likely needed

Structural

  • Every 5-10 years: Reapply linseed oil to exposed timber
  • At 15-20 years: Inspect wire lashings, replace if corroded
  • At 30-40 years: Assess timber condition for replacement

Seasonal

  • Spring: Full structural inspection, repair any winter damage
  • Summer: Maintain defensible space, check ventilation
  • Autumn: Clean gutters, prepare for rainy season
  • Winter: Monitor for leaks, check drainage function
